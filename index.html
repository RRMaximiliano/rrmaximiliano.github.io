Welcome

I am currently working as a econ research assistant at The World Bank, Development Impact Evaluation (DIME) unit where I have been evaluation a cross-randomized experiment at both the village and individual level in Muyanza, Rwanda.

Before coming to DC, I worked as an RA at KDI School of Public Policy and Management in South Korea working on the long-term impact of the civil war in Nicaragua and on the evaluation of social programs also in Nicaragua. Simultaneously, I worked with Professor Nathaniel Lane of SoDa Labs @ Monash University and University of Oxford, constructing a novel dataset on assasination events of environmental activists. Finally, I was an RA to Professor Mauricio Romero, ITAM and Innovation for Poverty Action, in Mexico City where I was working on the long-term impacts of outsourcing schools in Liberia.

Overall, my research interests are development economics, impact evaluations, labor market, and violence. Notwithstanding that my work has been alongside several professors, I have also independently developed a portfolio of data projects that can be of help for future research, and, especially, for Nicaragua that can be found in the data tab of this website.

Please feel free to contact me at the links provided on this website.
